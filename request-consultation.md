# Request Consultation

TechNerd uses a discovery-first consultation process. The goal is to understand the business problem, security driver, systems involved, urgency, and likely engagement fit before any work is scoped.

This is especially important for governance, identity, audit readiness, remediation, and advisory work because the right engagement shape depends on context.

## What To Include

When requesting a consultation, include the details that are comfortable and relevant:

- **Name and organization:** Your name, organization, role, and preferred contact method.
- **Organization type:** Enterprise, mid-market, small business, nonprofit, professional services firm, healthcare practice, community organization, or other.
- **Primary need:** Governance, identity and access, audit readiness, risk review, policy or procedure support, cloud ownership, vendor review, roadmap planning, or small-organization security guidance.
- **Business driver:** Audit preparation, finding remediation, leadership concern, stale access, unclear ownership, compliance pressure, vendor requirement, security roadmap, or general improvement.
- **Systems involved:** Examples include Microsoft 365, Google Workspace, Active Directory, Entra ID, IAM tools, SaaS platforms, cloud resources, business applications, or evidence repositories.
- **Timeline or urgency:** Desired start date, audit date, remediation deadline, board or leadership milestone, or whether the request is exploratory.
- **Preferred engagement type:** Assessment, advisory session, workshop, documentation support, package, a la carte service, or custom consulting.
- **Known constraints:** Budget sensitivity, availability, stakeholder access, documentation gaps, system access limits, or nonprofit considerations.

## Good Fit Examples

- An audit is approaching and evidence expectations are unclear.
- Access reviews are happening, but ownership, review criteria, or remediation tracking are weak.
- Application or cloud resource ownership is fragmented.
- A small organization needs practical security priorities without enterprise overhead.
- Leadership needs a clear briefing, roadmap, or risk-based sequence of next steps.
- Policies or control narratives exist, but they do not clearly connect to operating evidence.

## Not The Right Fit

TechNerd is not currently positioned as an emergency incident response provider, 24/7 managed IT help desk, hardware repair service, consumer tech support provider, or formal audit/certification authority.

If a request falls outside current service areas, TechNerd may decline it or suggest a better type of provider.

## How Discovery Works

1. **Initial request:** You send a brief description of the need, context, timeline, and systems involved.
2. **Fit review:** TechNerd reviews whether the request matches current service areas and availability.
3. **Discovery conversation:** If there appears to be a fit, the first conversation clarifies goals, stakeholders, scope, risks, and constraints.
4. **Scope recommendation:** TechNerd recommends a package, a la carte service, advisory path, or custom proposal.
5. **Clear agreement before work:** Work begins only after scope, deliverables, responsibilities, pricing approach, and timing are clear.
